YOGA AND ITS BENIFITS..

Malasana

The name Malasana is used for various squatting asanas in hatha yoga and modern yoga as exercise. Malasana is used for a squatting pose with the feet together and the back rounded with multiple hand placement svariations.

Benefits :

  • Malasana improves balance,concentration, and focus.
  • It also increase circulationand blood flow in the pelvis, which can help regulate sexual energy.
  • Malasana is very helpful for pregnant women and later help in childbirth.

Plank & Bridge

The plank (also called a front hold, hover, or abdominal bridge) is an isometric core strength exercise that involves maintaining a position similar to a push-up for the maximum possible time. Each time you visit this grounding pose you'll build abdominal strength and power in your arms and wrists. You'll also strengthen the muscles surrounding the spine, which improves posture.

Benefits :

  • Planks can help increase your flexibility.
  • Planks can help improve your posture
  • Planks activate more muscles

Child's Pose

Bālāsana, Child's Pose, or Child's Resting Pose is a kneeling asana in modern yoga as exercise. Balasana is a counter asana for various asanas and is usually practiced before and after Sirsasana.

Benefits :

  • Stretching: Child's pose gently stretches your spine, thighs, hips, and ankles.
  • Relaxation: Along with deep breathing exercises, child's pose can calm your mind, reducing anxiety and fatigue.
  • Blood circulation: Child's pose can increase blood circulation your head.

Butterfly

Butterfly Pose is suitable for all levels, so it’s a useful addition to most yoga routines. Because the posture improves flexibility and reduces tension, it’s ideal for people who have tight hips due to excessive sitting or high intensity workouts.

Benefits :

  • Butterfly Pose helps loosen up your low back, hips, and inner thighs, which may ease discomfort and help you feel better overall.
  • It can also have a calming, relaxing effect, which may help you manage and let go of stress.
  • Eases stress and reduces depression.

Upward facing dog

Upward dog involves placing your weight on your hands and lifting your legs off of the yoga mat as your chest reaches forward through your shoulders, prompting a deep bend in the low back.

Benefits :

  • Stimulates the abdominal muscles.
  • Strengthens back muscles.
  • Stretches wrists and tones arm muscles.

Apanasana

Apanasana is a relaxing supine asana often performed at the end of a yoga sequence. Apana is Sanskrit for "downward-moving life force" and asana means "pose" or "posture." As the name implies, this posture is thought to help move toxins downward, through and out of the body.

Benefits :

  • Stretches and stabilizes the pelvis and low back and can reduce lower back pain.
  • Reduces excessive anger, excitement, anxiety and high blood pressure.
  • Helps with muscle guarding, hyperlordosis, sciatic nerve impingement muscle spasm, spinal stenosis, and disc herniation.

Cobbler Pose

Cobbler's Pose (Baddha Konasana) is an essential pose for beginners. You can expect to see this pose often in yoga class. It's a good basic stretch that almost anyone can do, even if it takes a few props to get there. If your hips are tight, make sure to sit up on a folded blanket or two to raise your seat. You'll be surprised how this trick allows you to sit up straighter with much more comfort.

Benefits :

  • Stimulates abdominal organs, ovaries and prostate gland, bladder, and kidneys.
  • Stimulates the heart and improves general circulation.
  • Stretches the inner thighs, groins, and knees.

Candle Pose

Lie on your back and lift your legs, one at a time, bending your knees, into the air above you. Then point your toes. Your feet are now the flame on your candle! Ask a friend to blow on your toes as if they are blowing out your candle.

Benefits :

  • Builds strength and flexibility.
  • Builds strength and flexibility.
  • Can improve digestion system.

Danurasana

Dhanurasana is a back bending asana in hatha yoga and modern yoga as exercise. Those who suffer from excessive problems of spinal cord and disc should not perform this pose. If you suffer from severe stomach related problems then don't perform this pose.

Benefits :

  • It opens up shoulder blades.
  • It strengthening of back and abdominal muscles.
  • It helps reduce constipation.

Halasana

Halasana or Plough pose is an inverted asana in hatha yoga and modern yoga as exercise. Its variations include Karnapidasana with the knees by the ears, and Supta Konasana with the feet wide apart.

Benefits :

  • Practicing Halasana enhances flexibility, which improves muscle and joint mobility
  • Halasana also stimulates digestion, so it may be useful for constipation.
  • Halasana boosts circulation, improves blood pressure, and lowers blood sugar levels, which is helpful for diabetes management

Uttanasana

Uttanasana or Standing Forward Bend pose is an important and simple stretching yoga pose that contains a variety of therapeutic advantages. In the Standing Forward Bend Yoga posture, your head hangs below the heart allowing fresh oxygen-rich blood to flow to your brain.

Benefits :

  • It maintains the functions of liver, kidneys and spleen.
  • Gives Strengths to the knees and spine also.
  • Helps to improves posture and alignment.
